hometown

Pulaski is a small town nestled in the mountains of Southwest Virginia. Named for Count Pulaski, a Revolutionary War hero, Pulaski officially became a town proper in 1886. The early days boomed with businesses such as mineral processing/smelting, a passenger railway line, and the furniture industry. Currently the county's main attractions focus on the outdoor lifestyle. Claytor Lake State Park offers beautiful scenery for fishing, boating, or a weekend get-away in a lakeside cabin. The New River State Trail, which runs 57 miles, is perfect for those who like to go biking, hiking, or camping. Pulaski also sports the home field of the New York Yankees' Appalachian League affiliate in Calfee Park, one of the oldest historical stadiums still used in America.
